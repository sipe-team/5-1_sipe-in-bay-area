# Week 3

## Discussion Note

The main idea was that makers need long, uninterrupted blocks of time to focus, while managers often work in fragmented schedules filled with meetings and conversations.

One participant who manages a small team explained that their role combines both management and coding. They do not spend much time in formal meetings, but they are constantly interrupted by conversations, which affects focus. They felt that the whole team may effectively be working on a manager’s schedule because everyone interrupts one another.

Several others shared their own experiences with meetings:

One participant said they have two daily meetings, including a regular sync with Hyundai Motor.
Summer said her team has only a few formal meetings per week, but many of them feel unproductive because they lack clear purpose, documents, or conclusions. She found them frustrating because they reduce coding time and delay going home.
Matthew described a startup culture with minimal meetings, mainly daily stand-ups. His team tried to protect maker time by relying on documentation, task tools like Linear or Jira, and informal discussion during meals instead of interrupting work.
Stella described the most severe meeting burden. She has daily, weekly, and random ad hoc product meetings, often around three a day. She feels these meetings break up her workday so much that it becomes hard to focus, especially when a morning meeting is followed closely by lunch. She also noted that if her calendar appears open, others assume she is available.

The group then discussed whether long focus time is still as necessary now that AI tools like Codex and Claude can write code. Speaker 1 argued that AI reduces the need for long uninterrupted focus because coding can continue while the engineer does something else. Manager 1 partly agreed, saying that AI makes context switching easier than before, but also felt that concentration still matters, especially when handling many products at once. Summer said she does not naturally focus for very long and prefers a Pomodoro-like rhythm with breaks, but she still dislikes meetings because they interrupt both her focus time and her rest time.

Finally, the discussion turned to management as a career path. Speaker 1 said they do not want to be a manager because their company culture is meeting-heavy, and they previously found team management stressful. They said they enjoy solving difficult technical problems much more than managing people, and another participant pointed out that they seemed visibly happier when talking about coding.

Overall, the group agreed that unnecessary meetings reduce productivity, especially for engineers who need focused maker time. They also showed mixed views on whether AI changes this dynamic, but most still valued uninterrupted work time and expressed frustration with meeting-heavy cultures.


## Overall Feedback 

### 1. 한국어 직역(Konglish) 탈출하기
한국어의 뉘앙스를 영어로 그대로 옮기려다 보니 나온 재미있는 표현들을 훨씬 자연스러운 영어식 사고로 바꿔봅니다.

* **"She was the PM who was drinking honey."** ➡️ **"She was slacking off."** 또는 **"She had a cushy job."**
    * '꿀 빤다'는 직역하면 원어민에게 의미가 전달되지 않습니다. 농땡이를 피운다면 'slacking off', 편하고 쉬운 포지션에 있다면 'having a cushy job'이 자연스럽습니다.
* **"Table talk (Taksanggongnon)."** ➡️ **"Going around in circles."** 또는 **"All talk and no action."**
    * 탁상공론이라는 뉘앙스를 살리려면 "We just go around in circles (계속 제자리걸음만 한다)"가 아주 훌륭한 회화 표현입니다.
* **"This is this, that is that!"** ➡️ **"That's a separate issue."**
    * "이건 이거고 저건 저거지"라는 말은 "그건 별개의 문제지(That's a separate issue)"라고 표현하는 것이 훨씬 매끄럽습니다.
* **"My brain capacity is small."** ➡️ **"I have a short attention span."**
    * 집중력이 짧다는 의미로 쓰셨다면, 뇌 용량보다는 'attention span(주의 집중 시간)'을 활용하는 것이 원어민다운 표현입니다.

### 2. 프로페셔널한 비즈니스 & 오피스 실무 영어
회사 생활, 미팅, 업무 일정과 관련된 표현들을 한 단계 업그레이드해 줍니다.

* **"If you can’t do this, your grade is down."** ➡️ **"You'll get a bad performance review."**
    * 회사에서의 '고과(grade)'나 평가는 보통 'performance review'나 'evaluation'으로 표현합니다.
* **"Just throw the meetings."** ➡️ **"Just throw a meeting on my calendar."**
    * 캘린더에 그냥 회의를 꽂아버린다는 뉘앙스는 뒤에 'on my calendar'를 덧붙여주면 훨씬 자연스럽습니다.
* **"She will gone early."** ➡️ **"She is going on maternity leave soon."**
    * 임신 중인 PM이 자리를 비우게 되는 상황이라면, 단순히 일찍 간다기보다 '출산 휴가를 간다(maternity leave)'라고 명확히 표현하는 것이 비즈니스 매너에 맞습니다.
* **"Context switching price"** ➡️ **"Context switching cost"**
    * 컨텍스트 스위칭에 따르는 '비용'을 말할 때는 price보다는 **cost**라는 단어를 실무적으로 훨씬 많이 사용합니다.
* **"Because of that meeting, you have leftover work."** ➡️ **"Meetings leave me with a backlog of work."** * '남은 일'을 leftover(주로 음식에 쓰임)보다 **backlog**(밀린 업무)로 표현하거나, "I have to work overtime(야근해야 해)"으로 풀어 말하는 것이 좋습니다.
* **"Not a clearly Specifies purpose."** ➡️ **"Without a clear purpose."** 또는 **"No specific agenda."**
    * 명확한 안건이 없는 회의는 'no specific agenda'로 깔끔하게 표현할 수 있습니다.
* **"Not like we have to grab a room."** ➡️ **"We don't need to book a meeting room."**
    * 회의실을 잡다/예약하다는 "book a room" 또는 "reserve a room"을 주로 사용합니다.

### 3. 매끄러운 일상 대화 & 디테일 다듬기
작은 전치사나 단어 선택의 변화만으로도 문장의 흐름이 훨씬 부드러워지는 표현들입니다.

* **"Somebody have C-to-C cable?"** ➡️ **"Does anyone have a C-to-C cable?"**
    * 무언가를 빌리거나 찾을 때는 'Somebody'보다 'anyone'을 사용하여 의문문 형태로 묻는 것이 좋습니다.
* **"Please take me a picture."** ➡️ **"Could you take a picture of me?"**
    * "Take a picture" 뒤에 "of 사람" 형태로 써야 자연스럽습니다.
* **"I really love to immerse on work."** ➡️ **"I really love to immerse myself in work."** 또는 **"I love getting in the zone."**
    * immerse는 'immerse oneself in' 형태로 씁니다. 캐주얼하게 '초집중 상태에 빠지다'라고 할 때는 **'get in the zone'**을 강력히 추천합니다.
* **"Take a water or walking around."** ➡️ **"Grab some water or take a walk."**
    * 물을 마실 때는 'grab some water', 산책은 'take a walk'가 입에 착 붙는 짝꿍 표현입니다.
* **"Everyone ran away work with her."** ➡️ **"Everyone avoids working with her."**
    * 도망가다(ran away)보다 "기피하다/피하다(avoid working with)"라고 쓰는 것이 적절합니다.
* **"I want to go home fast."** ➡️ **"I want to get off work early."** 또는 **"I want to leave on time."**
    * 집에 "빨리 뛰어간다"는 느낌보다는 "조기 퇴근하다(get off work early)" 혹은 "칼퇴하다(leave on time)"로 표현해 보세요.
