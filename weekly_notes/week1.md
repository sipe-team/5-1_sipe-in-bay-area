### Stella

I was worried because it was our first session, but I was surprised that everyone had so much to say and that we almost ran out of time. It made me feel that we would be able to have fun discussions in English going forward.

Speaking is important, but I also realized that handling situations where the other person does not understand you is harder than I expected. In Korean, I can just repeat myself, but in English I suddenly started wondering whether they could not understand me because of my pronunciation. That made me panic for a moment.

The words I especially struggled to pronounce were `judge` (/dʒʌdʒ/) and `chose` (/tʃoʊz/).

Because I had to focus on both listening and speaking, my shoulders and neck were really sore after the session. Still, I felt proud that we were somehow able to keep the conversation going.

Another memorable part was looking at job descriptions from foreign companies. I usually focus on Korean companies, so reading overseas JDs helped me better understand current development trends and the direction each company is aiming for.

### Mattew

It is not easy to create an environment where you are forced to speak only English, especially for free, so I really liked that this session made that possible. It also made me feel that we could keep enjoying the sessions in the future.

If Korean were allowed even occasionally, we would probably end up depending on it. Thanks to the team lead's intention to block that from the start, I think we will be able to improve a lot over the coming weeks.

When unexpected questions came up, it was hard to organize my thoughts and explain clearly what I wanted to say.
